Claimed there was damage on the hinge cover (opposite side) preventing the inner screen protector replacement. The damage they are claiming, was not there when sent off. Required $99 outer assembly replacement for "pre-existing damage."

Protect Yourself

Red Flags to Watch For

Learn from these experiences - here's what to look out for when using Samsung Mail-In Repair Service.

üì∏

Before You Mail Your Device

  • Take detailed photos/videos of your device from ALL angles
  • Document every scratch, mark, and imperfection
  • Take photos in good lighting, multiple angles
  • Record IMEI, serial number, and device condition
  • Keep backup copies in multiple locations
üìù

During the Repair Process

  • Get repair authorization and quote in writing
  • Save ALL emails and support transcripts
  • Note names, dates, and times of all calls
  • Request tracking updates regularly
  • Don't accept vague explanations for delays
üîç

When You Receive It Back

  • Inspect IMMEDIATELY before signing
  • Compare to your before photos/videos
  • Document any NEW damage with photos/videos
  • Test all functions before accepting
  • Report issues within 24 hours in writing
üö®

Major Red Flags

  • Claims of "pre-existing damage" you didn't report
  • Unexpected charges without prior approval
  • Long delays without clear explanation
  • Difficulty reaching support or getting callbacks
  • Being transferred repeatedly between departments
  • Pressure to accept device "as-is"
