üìö Background: Why I Sought Manufacturer Service

For foldable devices like the Galaxy Z Fold series, the inner screen protector is considered a consumable component that can wear over time. Samsung publicly advertises a service policy offering one complimentary inner screen protector replacement, with subsequent replacements available for $20.

Samsung's official guidance advises customers not to remove the inner screen protector themselves, citing potential risks to the device and warranty coverage. This guidance, combined with the advertised service policy, led me to believe that sending the device to an authorized service provider was the safest and most appropriate course of action.

Q

Why didn't you just replace the screen protector yourself?

A

Samsung's official guidance advises against customers removing the inner screen protector themselves, citing potential risks to the device and warranty concerns. Samsung also advertises a service policy specifically for screen protector replacement (one free, then $20 each). I followed Samsung's recommended process and used their authorized service channels, believing this was the safest approach for a nearly $2,000 device.

Q

Why is the adhesive protrusion such a concern?

A

Adhesive protrusion indicates improper reassembly. It can create several problems: pressure on internal components that may cause future failures, compromised sealing that reduces dust/water protection, visible evidence of poor workmanship on a premium device, and uncertainty about what other corners may have been cut during reassembly. On a precision device like a foldable phone, attention to detail is critical.

Q

Why not just send the phone back in for another repair?

A

I have completely lost confidence in Samsung's service process. I sent in a device with a minor screen protector issue. I received inconsistent communication, was charged $99 for a repair I didn't originally need, and got the phone back with multiple new areas of damage. Samsung has offered no accountability, only to create another service ticket and repeat the process. I have no reason to believe a second service attempt would be any different, and I risk further damage to my device.

Q

What makes you think the damage happened during service and not during shipping?

A

Several factors: (1) The shipping package showed no external damage; (2) The device was properly secured in packaging; (3) The pattern of damage‚Äîparticularly the inner bezel damage and adhesive protrusion‚Äîis more consistent with disassembly/reassembly issues than shipping trauma; (4) Shipping damage typically affects external corners and edges, not internal bezel areas and adhesive application; (5) UPS packaged the device per Samsung's explicit instructions, following their procedures.

Q

Have you considered pursuing legal action?

A

I am currently pursuing resolution through regulatory channels (State Attorney General) and continuing to document all aspects of this case. I am attempting to resolve this through appropriate escalation channels before considering any legal options. My preference is for Samsung to acknowledge the issue and provide a fair resolution without requiring legal intervention.

Q

What are you doing now while waiting for resolution?

A

I am continuing to use the damaged device as I have no alternative. I have applied an aftermarket screen protector to the front display to mask the scratch. I monitor the adhesive protrusion for any signs of worsening separation. I am maintaining detailed documentation and pursuing all available regulatory and escalation channels. I remain open to a good-faith resolution from Samsung at any time.
